**Regulatory Trends Analysis**

The global regulatory framework for AI in healthcare primarily regulates artificial intelligence through Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) classifications under existing medical device laws, emphasizing risk-based approaches across jurisdictions. The International Telecommunication Union (ITU) provides foundational guidance through its 2022/2023 publication "Regulatory Considerations on Artificial Intelligence for Health," emphasizing six key areas: transparency and documentation across the product lifecycle, risk management addressing intended use and cybersecurity, data quality with bias mitigation, clinical validation through independent testing, post-market surveillance, and ethical governance promoting equity.

Similarly, the International Medical Device Regulators Forum (IMDRF) establishes Good Machine Learning Practice (GMLP) through its 2025 document, outlining 10 guiding principles for AI/ML-enabled medical devices. In addition, the IMDRF efforts promote harmonization by standardizing the risk categorization of SaMD and total product lifecycle (TPLC) oversight, addressing adaptive algorithms, cybersecurity, and post-market surveillance.
